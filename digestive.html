INTRODUCTION

Humanity has advanced beyond any other creature thanks to our growing awareness of
the functioning of our bodies. We've even done enough research on our bodies to be
able to sustain them in spacesuits. Isn't that incredible?

But we have to endure before we can move forward. Since it is responsible for absorbing
nutrients and giving us the "fuel" we need to work and do even the most basic tasks, our
digestive system is vital to human health. We run the possibility of irreversibly harming
a significant aspect of our lives if we handle it carelessly, and we won't even be able to
determine whether a particular symptom is alarming.

By completing this exercise, we can better picture and comprehend the digestive systems in
our bodies and get a "look" at the eerie beauty hidden within even the most ordinary objects.
For instance, what happens to the food we eat?

CONCLUSION:

Even the most fundamental function of eating isn't all that easy since organisms are so complicated. It's not easy
even to put food in our mouths! The food must be physically torn apart by our teeth, shielded from harm by saliva
and aiding in swallowing, and then formed into a form that is easily transferred by our tongue to the esophagus.

After being chemically broken down by our stomach, which churns and mixes with acid strong enough to melt metal,
our food is thereafter gradually sent to the small intestine, where most of the nutrients and water are absorbed.
It has to be folded, wrinkled, and lengthy in order to have precisely the right amount of surface area for food
digestion. The stuff will only be sent to the rectum in the form of feces after the large intestine has done its
best to eliminate as much water from it as possible. And then it emerges from the anus as waste material

It is critical that we comprehend the functioning of our digestive systems in order to identify any problems as
soon as they arise. Your small intestine may be collapsing as a result of the "stomach flu." You may have a "bit
of extra watery stool" if something is wrong with your big intestine. Having firsthand observation of this aids in
our ability to better image and comprehend our body.
