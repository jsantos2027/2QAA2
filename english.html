Love, Longing, Leaving

A collection of sonnets from our administration
that aim to capture emotions of love and longing
and depict the pain and strength of leaving. The
sonnets are the "unsent letters" for the reason
that the lyrics of the songs contain the hidden
feelings...feelings they are too scared to express.

JOSHUA ANGELO SANTOS

PERTRARCHAN SONNET: Trapped in the Labyrinth of my mind

Sonnetization of the song "Promise" by Laufey

Temptation I tried to stop until my last breath
Defeat is my destined goal
It hurts to always play the main role
Without you an emptiness like death
The equation of you I tried to answer with all my best
A solution that never came took its toll
Being doomed to fall apart plagued my body and soul
Questioning a departure that promised unending rest

The delusion of a promise I can no longer pursue
was to call you once again
In over my head, guilty of the mess I just made
My eyes betrayed me when I thought I saw you
Looking like you did back then
To see you on that avenue was a sight I forbade.

AUTHOR'S NOTE
This sonnet talks about a promise the speaker made and ended up breaking
not because of the other's persuasion of the speaker but because they
themself cannot let go of that person that even promises that they themself
made are broken.

- Joshua

SHAKEPEAREAN SONNET: Trapped in the Labyrinth of my mind

Sonnetization of the song "the grudge" by Olivia Rodrigo

The terrors each night about that cursed day in May
That life-changing phone call which in my head still lingers
The lines that you crossed an unchanging display
Took all my hopes and dreams and crushed them right between your fingers

The damage that you did, who am I to even judge?
Everything you did left me dead and wanting more
Love I held so dear, forever haunts me like a grudge
Your condescending voice will forever be my great war

My mask of a brave face hides the agony inside
How could one do the things you did with such ease
I smile through the pain and tell myself “it's my pride”
You know I can’t let it go even if I prayed to God on my knees

I’ve lost my mind from trying for so long
It took all my strength to forgive you yet I don’t feel strong


AUTHOR'S NOTE
The grudge is such an ultimate "I still love you song" and I wanted to really
bring that emotion in this sonnet where in the start they are troubled for
a night but then that night turns into more night of painful reminiscence,
but even after being hurt by this person so many times they still want them back

- Joshua

YSABELLE MARGRETHE ALORRO

PERTRARCHAN SONNET: The Visions Of A Could Be

Sonnetization of the song "The Apartment We Won't Share" by NIKI

All the mayhems that could make a sad wife close the door;
From deciphering a mysterious soul's stares,
To building up bricks to cover up scandalous affairs;
Is every wonder and dream that I have ever bargained for.
Our swevens of the future are dreams I won't hope for anymore.
The drastic changes made have gone beyond repairs.
Your smirking lips and our make-believe children are the agonizing scenes my soul still bares.
And in the end, the woman I shall be is the one that can't be by your side forevermore.

The untold tale in the deepest vault of my mind is my monumental fantasy.
A lavish feeling of passion that lost track will make its way back.
And the vivacious soul of a wife I won't be is undoubtedly for the best.
Your demons I won't meet are words that are kept but not with me.
Of all probabilities, it is certain that love won't be your lack.
It does not align with my truest of desires, but crucial for two hearts in desperate call for rest.

AUTHOR'S NOTE
The sonnet aims to captivate the feeling of a person's longing and their
heart's pining for a future they dreamed of or plans that they have "established"
and written in the pages of their journal without knowing that whatever's written
in the stars is entirely different. All the feelings captured in this were feelings
that gave me a hard time to put into words as the devastation that the what ifs and
could've beens bring is too heavy to be easily interpreted and turned into a piece
of literature.

- Ysa

SHAKEPEAREAN SONNET: A lonely lover's wishes

Sonnetization of the song "the 1" by Taylor Swift

The brand new endeavours of a lonely lover.
Image of the old flame that's still so refined.
A wondrous film that never got the chance to uncover,
Shall stay as an idea and a mere creation of the mind.

But then I guess, both of us shall never know,
What would happen if the weary heart's wishes became true?
Would we be something as we always thought so?
Would the emptiness be filled by you?

Sweet as the rosè flowing,
I could have been your chosen family.
Bitter as the agonizing feeling,
Should've been, could've been, but will never be me.

If one thing had been different, maybe things would have been fun.
If one thing had been different, you would’ve been the one.


AUTHOR'S NOTE
Another sonnet about longing. However, the other one was more like a "whatever's
written in the shore may not be written in the stars" and this one is more like a
"whatever's written in the stars may be rewritten". This sonnet encapsulates a lonely
lover's yearning for a future that SHOULD HAVE BEEN, but a tragedy led them to where
they are now hence the line, "if one thing had been different, you would've been the one".

- Ysa

RAINA RAYELLE BAUTISTA

PERTRARCHAN SONNET: Echoes of Goodbye

Sonnetization of the song "Oceans and Engines" by NIKI

In twined digits, minds entwined
Crying, pleading, "Stay, my love, let’s not bid our farewell"
A tear-stained calm, angst still there beneath the indigo's spell.
You whispered solace, "Darling, cease the cry; our path is to where the stars are aligned."
The present, where you're not familiar, like there is nothing to rewind.
Missing yesterday, when the passion's were parallel, when our affections did tell.
The sky forbidden, the snows fell, destiny not wanting to hear the ringing bell.
All I've known is long gone; even from miles away, there's none that I could find.

I'm ready; finally untangling the rope.
It's loose now; everything is free.
In the story I wrote, we have a different trope.
But this is the ending of the story, I think we both agree.
I'll be alright soon, I hope; someday I'll be able to cope.
Just let me grieve tonight, I plea.

AUTHOR'S NOTE
"Echoes of Goodbye" talks about learning how to let go. It shows that there's something good
about farewell. Though saying goodbye is tough, everyone is given time to grieve. I also want
to emphasize that there are things that end that are always for the better.

- Rai

SHAKEPEAREAN SONNET: Dare of Fate

Sonnetization of the song "Take A Chance With Me" by NIKI

In dulcet tones, his voice, a tender shroud, says my name,
I'd melt within, but fade away if he's too loud.
It’s funny how we’re so different, totally not the same.
I can see right through you, like clear skies, but you see me covered with clouds.

In passion's depths, he stirs my restless mind
His gaze, unconscious, meets my stare
Hope desperately, emotions unwind
Love overvalued, a burden we won't bear

In the end, it's a shame that we chose not to take the risk.
Oh, why can't we for once
Say what we want, express what we feel; let's not resist.
Oh, why can't you for once

Abandon the world's facade, sprint toward truth that you feel.
Let's be together and heal.


AUTHOR'S NOTE
Dare of Fate is a sonnet that is based on my experience. It is inspired by the song
"Take a Chance With Me," which I really relate to. As someone who likes her best friend,
I am really afraid to risk our friendship. My sonnet speaks about how I feel. Our friendship
is something that I treasure so much that it makes it hard for me to risk it. However, even if
it's too risky, it is still very worth it, and I'll be brave enough to take the risk.

- Rai
